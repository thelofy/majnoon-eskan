Capitals and cornices are among the most defining elements of visual identity in classical architecture. They connect columns to the building and establish the facade's horizontal line. In contemporary Iranian neoclassical architecture, precise CNC production enables projects once imaginable only in Europe.

## Three Main Capital Orders

### Doric

The simplest order — no base, with echinus and abacus. Suited to robust, masculine buildings.

### Ionic

With volutes at corners. More delicate, associated with graceful proportions in classical theory.

### Corinthian

Decorated with acanthus leaves — the most ornate order. Very common in contemporary Iranian luxury villas.

## Capital Components

```
Abacus (top slab)
    ↓
Echinus / Acanthus (decorative body)
    ↓
Necking (sometimes with collar)
    ↓
Column Shaft
```

In **composite capitals**, part of the element is fiber or concrete with stone cladding — reducing weight and cost.

## Cornice: Components and Function

Classical cornices consist of horizontal layers:

| Layer | Name | Function |
|-------|------|----------|
| Lower | Bed mould | Transition from wall |
| Middle | Corona | Main projection |
| Upper | Cyma recta | Decorative termination |

**Structural function:** Shedding rainwater from the wall  
**Decorative function:** Creating shadow and defining floors

## CNC Production of Capitals and Cornices

Production challenges:

- **Undercuts** at capital necks — requires 5-axis
- **Repeatability** — all capitals in a row must match
- **Weight** — large pieces need crane-rated packing

In [Villa Mollasadra](/en/projects/villa-mollasadra), composite capitals and multi-layer cornices were CNC-produced.

## Proportions

Vitruvius and Renaissance architects defined standard ratios:

- Doric capital height: 4/14 of column height
- Ionic capital height: 2/9 of column height
- Corinthian capital height: 1/6 of column height

In contemporary projects, these ratios are adapted to building scale.

## Installation

Capital and cornice installation notes:

1. **Mechanical connection** to beam or wall — not adhesive alone
2. **Expansion joints** in long cornice runs
3. **Drip slope** minimum 3 degrees
4. **Install sequence** bottom to top — cornice before final capital
