Pool coping and fountain stone elements are among the most demanding pieces in luxury villa projects. Exposed to water, chemicals, UV, and constant traffic, poor design and production cause staining, cracking, slip hazards, and water infiltration. In the [Traditional Pool](/en/special-works/traditional-pool) project, Eskan Stone designed and produced all pool and fountain stone elements.

## What Is Pool Coping?

Coping is the horizontal stone capping the top of the pool wall. Its roles:

- Covering concrete edges and preventing water ingress
- Creating seating surface and decoration
- Directing rainwater away from the pool

## Common Coping Profiles

| Profile | Application |
|---------|-------------|
| Bullnose | Residential pools — comfortable and safe |
| Square edge | Modern look — corners should be eased |
| Rebated | Wall-mounted — concealed joint |
| Drop face | Tall vertical edge — luxury appearance |

## Stone Selection for Pools

### Antique Stone and Travertine

Common in classical Iranian projects. Natural texture and warm tone harmonize with villa architecture.

**Note:** Travertine must be filled and sealed; brushed or honed finish for pool floors.

### Marble

Beautiful but higher maintenance. Better for coping than pool floor.

### Granite

Most durable for floor and edge. Flamed or bush-hammered finish for slip resistance.

## Technical Requirements

- **Coping thickness:** Minimum 30 mm, preferably 40 mm
- **Slope:** 1–2 degrees outward for drainage
- **Joints:** Chlorine-resistant epoxy or polyurethane
- **Slip resistance:** Minimum R11 friction for pool floors (DIN 51130)

## Related Fountain Elements

In combined pool and fountain projects:

## Eskan Stone Production Process

1. Receive pool drawings and exact dimensions
2. 3D model coping and steps
3. CNC cutting with ±1 mm tolerance
4. Surface finishing and slip control
5. Separate packing per coping row

## Maintenance

- Weekly wash with water and mild soap
- Avoid acid on marble and travertine
- Re-seal every 2–3 years
- Inspect joints after winter

## Conclusion

Pool coping and fountain elements should be coordinated with the stone manufacturer from initial design — not as a last-minute choice.
