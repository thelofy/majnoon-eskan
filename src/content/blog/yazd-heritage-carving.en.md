Yazd, a city with millennia of history in the heart of the Iranian desert, is one of the richest centers of architecture and craft in the Middle East. While Yazd is best known for brick architecture and iconic wind towers, stonework has played a vital if less visible role in historic buildings, mosques, hussainiyas, and contemporary villas. [Eskan Stone](/en/about) is based in Yazd and merges this heritage with modern CNC technology.

## Stone in Yazd Architecture

Stone in Yazd has been used primarily for:

- **Bases and columns** of important buildings and entrance portals
- **Paving** in courtyards and corridors
- **Quoins and ornament** combined with brick
- **Cisterns and qanats** — stone for water pressure resistance

Yazd architects have a long tradition of combining local materials: brick for walls, stone for structurally stressed points and high-traffic surfaces.

## Yazd Stone Carvers: A Living Craft

Contrary to common perception, stonework in Yazd has neither declined nor been confined to museums. Local master carvers hold skills passed down through generations:

- Carving Islamic geometric patterns
- Polishing and finishing stone in arid climate conditions
- Restoring stone elements in historic buildings

At Eskan Stone, these masters work alongside CNC operators — CNC produces main elements and hand carving completes final details.

## Local Stone and Yazd Production Advantages

Yazd is close to travertine quarries and building stone of central Iran. Production in Yazd offers:

- **Access to native materials** with texture harmonious to regional architecture
- **Desert climate expertise** — knowledge of stone performance in heat and aridity
- **Short supply chain** from quarry to factory
- **Export routes** via Bandar Abbas and transit roads to the Gulf

The [Villa Meybod](/en/projects/villa-meybod) project exemplifies local stone in a contemporary villa facade.

## From Tradition to Modern Projects

Neoclassical architecture in Yazd and surroundings — villas with capitals, cornices, and stone panels — has grown significantly. This style demands precise industrial production:

| Traditional element | Contemporary method |
|--------------------|---------------------|
| Hand-carved capital | CNC + hand finishing |
| Courtyard paving | Laser cutting + systematic layout |
| Facade ornament | 3D modeling + serial production |

[Villa Mollasadra](/en/projects/villa-mollasadra) is a leading example of this fusion.

## Preserving Heritage Through Innovation

Eskan Stone's approach preserves heritage while advancing industry:

- Training young workers in CAD/CAM alongside carving fundamentals
- Digitally archiving local ornamental patterns
- Collaborating with architects on designs rooted in Islamic geometry

## Conclusion

Yazd's stonework heritage is not only history — it is the foundation for contemporary architectural stone production in Iran and export to international markets.
