Choosing facade stone is one of the most critical architectural decisions in classical and luxury projects. Stone type, surface finish, panel thickness, and installation method directly affect durability, maintenance cost, and the building's visual identity. At [Eskan Stone](/en/about), with over two decades of experience producing architectural stone elements, this guide is based on international dimension-stone standards (including ASTM C1527 and EN 1469) and real project experience in Yazd and the Gulf region.

## Why Stone Selection Matters for Classical Facades

Classical facades depend on precise decorative elements — capitals, cornices, panels, and quoins. Unlike industrial cladding, natural stone has color and texture variation; each piece contributes to the building's character. Poor selection can cause yellowing, freeze-thaw cracking, or color mismatch between CNC-cut and hand-carved elements.

## Common Stone Types for Classical Facades

### Travertine

Travertine is among the most popular stones in classical Iranian and Mediterranean architecture. Its natural porosity reduces weight, and its warm texture pairs well with composite columns and cornices. In the [Villa Mollasadra](/en/projects/villa-mollasadra) project, travertine was used for the main facade and white marble for decorative details.

**Advantages:** More affordable than marble, natural texture, suited to hot dry climates  
**Technical notes:** Surface voids should be filled before installation; honed or brushed finishes are recommended for facades

### Marble

Marble symbolizes luxury in classical architecture. Its high hardness and polishability make it ideal for capitals, consoles, and ornamental details.

**Advantages:** Visual elegance, high durability, wide color range  
**Technical notes:** Sensitive to acid and acid rain; exterior use requires periodic sealing

### Antique Stone and Local Varieties

Antique cream stone has a warm tone and granular texture, widely used in traditional villas in Yazd and Isfahan. Local Yazd stone offers lower transport cost and harmony with regional architectural heritage.

## Technical Selection Criteria

| Criterion | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Water absorption | Below 0.5% (marble) to 2% (filled travertine) ideal for exteriors |
| Compressive strength | Minimum 12 MPa recommended for wall cladding |
| Freeze-thaw resistance | Essential in cold climates |
| Thickness | Facade panels: 30–50 mm; cornices and capitals per structural design |
| Surface finish | Honed, brushed, sandblasted — each affects reflectance and slip |

## Matching Stone to Architectural Style

- **Iranian-Roman classical:** Cream travertine + white marble accents
- **Modern neoclassical:** Uniform stone with honed texture and precise CNC lines
- **Contemporary luxury villa:** Two-texture combination (matte and polished) for visual depth

## Common Mistakes

- Choosing stone from catalog photos alone, without seeing full-scale samples
- Ignoring thickness differences between cornice and panel elements
- Using polished stone on south-facing facades without thermal reflection planning
- Lack of coordination between installer and CNC manufacturer

## Conclusion

The right stone for a classical facade harmonizes aesthetically with the architectural style and performs physically in local conditions. Travertine and marble remain the top choices for luxury projects in Iran and the Gulf.
