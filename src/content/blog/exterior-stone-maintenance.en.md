Exterior stone cladding is a long-term investment — with proper care it lasts decades; without it, even premium stone suffers staining, algae, cracking, and discoloration. This guide follows Natural Stone Institute (MIA+BSI) recommendations and Eskan Stone project experience in arid and humid climates.

## Facade Stone Degradation Factors

| Factor | Effect | Prevention |
|--------|--------|------------|
| Acid rain | Carbonate dissolution in marble | Sealing, appropriate stone choice |
| UV | Fading | Natural stone, not painted |
| Algae and mold | Green/black stains | Periodic cleaning |
| Freeze-thaw | Cracking in porous stone | Void filling, resistant stone |
| Salt (coastal) | Surface erosion | Sealing, fresh-water rinse |
| Urban pollution | Soot staining | Gentle pressure wash |

## Cleaning Stone Facades

### Correct Method

1. **Pre-wet** surface before cleaning
2. **Neutral pH detergent** (7–9) — not acidic
3. **Soft brush** or pad — no steel wool
4. **Rinse thoroughly** with clean water
5. **Air dry** — avoid steam cleaning

### What Not to Use

- Acid (muriatic acid, vinegar) — especially on marble
- High pressure on joints
- Coarse abrasives

## Sealing

Porous stone (travertine, antique stone) needs sealing every 2–5 years:

- **Impregnating sealer** — penetrates without changing appearance
- Apply in dry weather with 24 hours rain-free
- Test on small sample before full facade

Polished marble exteriors are more sensitive — annual sealing recommended.

## Algae and Mold Removal

In humid or shaded areas:

- Water + mild dish soap solution
- Soft brush and rinse
- Severe cases: stone-rated biocide per manufacturer
- **Never** direct chlorine on marble

## Cracks and Repair

Fine hairline cracks are usually non-structural — from thermal expansion. Wider cracks need structural review.

**Local repair:**
- Clean the crack
- Fill with color-matched resin/epoxy
- Finish flush with surface

For structural cracks, [contact Eskan Stone](/en/contact).

## Seasonal Maintenance

### Spring
- Full wash after winter
- Joint inspection

### Summer
- Check for stains and algae
- Seal if needed

### Autumn
- Remove leaves and debris
- Prepare for rain

### Winter
- In cold climates: check freeze damage
- Avoid salt on stone

## Maintenance by Stone Type

| Stone | Cleaning frequency | Sealing |
|-------|-------------------|---------|
| Travertine | Annual | Every 3–5 years |
| Marble | Every 6 months | Every 1–2 years |
| Granite | Annual | Every 5+ years |
| Antique stone | Annual | Every 3 years |

## Conclusion

Preventive maintenance reduces major repair cost. A simple annual program — clean + inspect + seal — preserves stone facades for generations.
