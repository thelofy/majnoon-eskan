Exporting architectural stone to international markets — especially Gulf countries — requires more than visual quality. Every piece must meet export standards for dimensions, packaging, documentation, and transport durability. With export experience including the [UAE Turkish Bath](/en/special-works/turkish-bath-uae) project, Eskan Stone maintains a comprehensive QC system for export production.

## Challenges in Building Stone Export

Stone is fragile and heavy. Main export risks include:

- **Breakage in transit** from inadequate packaging
- **Moisture and salt** in maritime environments
- **Customs delays** from incomplete documentation
- **Mismatch** of pieces upon arrival at site

## Technical Standards Applied

Eskan Stone export production aligns with:

| Standard | Application |
|----------|-------------|
| EN 1469 | Natural stone cladding — requirements |
| ASTM C97 | Water absorption and density |
| ASTM C99 | Compressive strength |
| ISO 9001 | Quality management system (target/partnership) |

Each stone batch is sampled for water absorption and compressive strength before production.

## Factory Quality Control Process

### 1. Incoming Material Inspection

- Color, texture, and crack review in stone blocks
- Match to client-approved sample

### 2. In-Process Control

- Digital caliper measurement of each piece
- Piece number and position recorded on installation map

### 3. Final Inspection

- Trial assembly for complex sets
- Photography of each piece before packing

## Export Packaging

Standard Eskan Stone packaging includes:

- **Wooden crates** with internal framing
- **Polyethylene foam** or surface protective wrap
- **Palletizing** with steel strapping and shrink wrap
- **Bilingual labels** (Persian/English) with project number, dimensions, and weight

For Gulf projects, additional moisture and salt protection is applied.

## Documentation and Logistics

Each export shipment includes:

- **Detailed packing list** with every piece number
- **Updated shop drawings** for installation
- **Certificate of origin** and commercial invoice
- **All-risk cargo insurance**

Transport runs from Yazd to Bandar Abbas, then by sea to Dubai, Abu Dhabi, and other Gulf destinations.

## Project Experience: UAE Turkish Bath

In the UAE Turkish Bath project:

- Over 150 marble and travertine pieces were produced
- Each piece was packed with a serial number
- Technical team provided on-site assembly guidance

## Tips for International Clients

- Review stone samples on site before final order
- Allow 8–12 weeks minimum for production and shipping
- Approve shop drawings before production — post-production changes are costly

## Conclusion

Export quality means a piece that travels from our Yazd factory to a site in Dubai or elsewhere without damage and with full dimensional compliance.
