CNC (Computer Numerical Control) technology has revolutionized architectural stonework. What once required weeks of hand carving can now be produced in equipped factories with millimeter precision and full repeatability. At [Eskan Stone](/en/capabilities), 5-axis CNC machines are the backbone of classical facade elements, composite capitals, and custom details.

## How CNC Stonework Works

The CNC process has three main stages:

1. **CAD modeling:** 3D design of the stone element in architectural software
2. **CAM programming:** Converting the model into toolpaths for the machine
3. **Machining:** Automated cutting on stone with X, Y, Z and two rotational axes

**5-axis machines** enable carving of curved surfaces and complex undercuts — such as capital necks or multi-layer cornices — without repeatedly repositioning the workpiece.

## CNC Advantages Over Traditional Methods

| Advantage | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Precision | ±0.5 mm tolerance on standard projects |
| Speed | 200+ unique pieces produced in weeks |
| Repeatability | Every piece is an exact copy of the design |
| Waste reduction | Optimized nesting on stone blocks |
| Safety | Lower injury risk for workers on heavy cuts |

## CNC Applications at Eskan Stone

- **Classical capitals and cornices:** Composite pattern carving with ornamental detail
- **Facade panels:** Anchor holes and concealed channel cutting
- **Fountains and pools:** Curved edges and integrated stone steps
- **Interiors:** Custom CNC wall cladding — as in the [Pardebaf Showroom](/en/projects/pardebaf-showroom)

In the [Villa Mollasadra](/en/projects/villa-mollasadra) project, over 200 unique stone pieces were produced on 5-axis CNC and shipped for installation.

## Quality Control in CNC Production

Eskan Stone QC includes:

- **Laser measurement** of piece dimensions after cutting
- **Visual matching** of color and texture within project batches
- **Trial assembly** for complex capitals and cornices
- **Documentation** of each piece with project serial number

## Limitations and Hand-Carving Integration

CNC does not replace everything. Fine relief detail, special patination, and some artistic restoration still require master stone carvers. The best results come from **combining CNC and hand work** — the same approach rooted in Yazd's carving heritage and continued in modern production.

## The Future of CNC in Stone

Emerging trends include:

- **3D scanning** of historic elements for accurate reproduction
- **FEM simulation** for thin-element strength prediction
- **Robotics** in packing and stone handling lines

## Conclusion

CNC brings precision, speed, and scalability to architectural stonework, enabling complex classical facades with predictable cost and timeline.
