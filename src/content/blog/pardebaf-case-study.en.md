The Pardebaf showroom in Tehran exemplifies how stone becomes the primary canvas for brand identity in commercial space. Unlike exterior facades, luxury interior stonework demands precision in texture, light, and customer experience. Eskan Stone handled design, CNC production, and execution.

## Project Summary

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Location | Tehran, Iran |
| Year | 2024 |
| Type | Commercial — showroom |
| Stone | Grey marble and travertine |
| Scope | Interior stonework — walls and floors |

[View full project](/en/projects/pardebaf-showroom)

## Design Challenge

The client required a space that:

- **Reflects Pardebaf brand** identity
- Feels **luxurious** but not cold or inaccessible
- Remains **practical** for customer traffic and product display
- **Harmonizes** with interior color palette

The project architect chose stone as the "primary canvas" — not merely cladding material.

## Stone Solution

### Large Wall Cladding

Large-format wall panels were cut at the Yazd factory. CNC cutting ensured dimensional accuracy for site installation without field adjustment.

**Large panel advantage:** Fewer visible joints — more unified appearance.

### Texture Combination

- **Grey marble** — matte and polished sections for visual depth
- **Travertine** — accent points and texture contrast

This two-texture combination broke monotony and created focal points for product display.

### CNC Details

CNC-cut details — edges, channels, and connections — preserved modern identity alongside natural stone.

## Production Process

1. **Architectural drawings** and stone sample approved
2. **3D modeling** of numbered panels
3. **CNC production** at Eskan Stone Yazd factory
4. **Quality control** — color and dimension match
5. **Packaging** — each panel separately
6. **Transport** to Tehran and installation

## Project Lessons

### For Interior Designers

- Introduce stone early in design — not at final stage
- View stone samples in actual showroom lighting
- Specify joints on drawings

### For Clients

- Factory production = consistent quality
- 4–6 weeks production time for this scale
- Installer coordination with manufacturer is essential

## Results

The Pardebaf showroom became a space that:

- Engages customers with brand quality from entry
- Displays curtain products on a luxury backdrop
- Lasts years without major repair

## Conclusion

Commercial interior stonework succeeds when stone tells the brand story — not just covers walls. The Pardebaf showroom demonstrates this principle.
