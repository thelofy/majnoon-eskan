A custom stone fountain is the focal point of many luxury villas and entrance spaces. Unlike prefabricated fountains, custom design enables full harmony with building architecture, desired water flow, and appropriate stone selection. In the [Zein Fountain](/en/special-works/zein-fountain) project, Eskan Stone handled all stages from design through installation.

## Stage 1: Concept and Planning

### Defining Purpose

- **Decorative:** Wall or central fountain in entrance
- **Acoustic:** Water sound for ambiance
- **Symbolic:** Architectural element in courtyard or garden

### Initial Parameters

| Parameter | Key question |
|-----------|--------------|
| Location | Interior or exterior? Shade or sun? |
| Dimensions | Height, width, basin depth |
| Budget | Affects stone type and complexity |
| Style | Classical, modern, Iranian |

## Stage 2: Architectural and 3D Design

The designer, with Eskan Stone team:

1. **Sketch and moodboard** — overall form direction
2. **3D modeling** — precise view from all angles
3. **Rendering** — simulation in actual project environment
4. **Client approval** — before production

In Zein Fountain, classical design with white marble was executed for a villa entrance in Isfahan.

## Stage 3: Water Engineering and Hydraulics

A fountain must work correctly — not just look beautiful:

- **Flow calculation** — pump with adequate capacity
- **Concealed piping** — inside stone or behind wall
- **Collection basin** — sufficient volume for circulation
- **Overflow and drainage** — splash prevention

Eskan Stone technical team coordinates with project mechanical engineer so water holes and channels are integrated in CNC stage.

## Stage 4: Stone Selection

For fountains:

- **White marble:** Classic, polished, suited to interiors
- **Travertine:** Warm texture, requires sealing
- **Granite:** Durable outdoors

**Note:** Stone in constant water contact must be sealed. Do not use unfilled porous stone.

## Stage 5: CNC Production

Fountain pieces typically include:

- Back panel or main body
- Basin edge
- Central ornamental element
- Base and platform

Precise CNC cutting for:

- Water flow channels
- Pipe holes
- Piece connections without open joints

## Stage 6: Installation

Typical install sequence:

1. Prepare concrete substrate
2. Waterproofing and piping
3. Install stone pieces bottom to top
4. Seal joints
5. Test flow and adjust pump

## Stage 7: Maintenance

- Monthly limescale cleaning (hard water areas)
- Pump and filter inspection
- Annual re-sealing outdoors

## Common Mistakes

- Design without hydraulic calculation
- Wrong stone for outdoor exposure
- Ignoring winter freeze risk
- No pump access for service

## Conclusion

A successful custom fountain results from architect, water engineer, and stone producer collaboration. Starting this cooperation early reduces cost and delay.
